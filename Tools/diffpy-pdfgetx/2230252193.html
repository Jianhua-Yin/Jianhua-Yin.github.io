diffpy.pdfgetx: Advanced Programs for Converting Powder Diffraction Data into Total Scattering Pair Distribution Functions

Posted on 07-15-2023 In Tools , diffpy.pdfgetx Waline: Word count in article: 2.3k Reading time ≈ 9 mins.

diffpy.pdfgetx3 is an advanced and powderful software for converting Powder Diffraction data to Total Scattering atomic Pair Distribution Funtions. In addtion, it can also help us to extrat the PDFs from small-angle scattering(SAS). The program include three command line programs PDFgetx3, PDFgetN3 and PDFgetS3 for processing X-ray, Nertron, and Small-angle diffraction data respectively.

Overview

PDF data analysis typically consists of three parts:

  • Obtaining one-dimensional diffraction data (e.g., using Fit2D, Dioptas)
  • Extracting the total scattering pair distribution function (PDF) from the diffraction data (e.g., using diffpy.pdfgetx)
  • Modeling analysis of the PDFs (e.g., using RMCProfile, DISCUS).

diffpy.pdfgetx can readily process hundreds of powder diffraction patterns within a few seconds. With an ad hoc data reduction and correction algorithm, pdfgetxns3 requires little user input, generates PDFs in a fraction of a second, and can be straightforwardly automated to batch process thousands of PDFs.

Tips:
Despite the tricky definition, it is worth remembering that S(Q) is nothing other than the powder diffraction pattern that has been corrected for experimental artifacts and suitably normalized.
Corrections include, but are not limited to, background subtraction, polarization, self-absorption, multiple scattering, and Compton scattering.

Coherent scattering (also known as Rayleigh, unmodified, classical or elastic scattering) is one of three forms of photon interaction which occurs when the energy of the x-ray or gamma photon is small in relation to the ionization energy of the atom (for instance, during mammography). Such low-energy radiation triggers all the electrons in an atom to vibrate at a similar frequency to the incident photon. Upon interacting with the attenuating medium, the photon does not have enough energy to liberate the electron from its bound state (i.e. the photon energy is well below the binding energy of the electron) so no energy transfer occurs. There is no energy deposition and thus no dose resulting from coherent scattering. The only change is a change of direction (scatter) of the photon, hence 'unmodified' scatter. Coherent scattering is not a major interaction process encountered in radiography at the energies normally used.

The S(Q) should oscillate around and the approach unity as Q increasing to infinity.

The coherent intensity (the features) in S(Q) dies out with increasing Q due to the Debye–Waller factor, which comes from thermal and quantum zero-point motion of the atoms as well as any static displacive disorder in the material.
