What is total scattering?

Background

Traditional crystallographic approach to structure determination is insufficient or fails for1

  • Non crystalline materials
  • Disordered material (the interesting properties are oftern governed by the defects or local structure!)
  • Nanostructures (Well defined local structure, but long-range order limited to few nanometers.[poorly defined Bragg peaks])

Total scattering = Bragg scattering + Diffuse scattering

Bragg scattering: Information about the average structure, e.g. average positions, displacement parameters and occupancies. (Materials with different elemental occupancies can exhibit the same pattern.)

Diffuse scattering: Information about two-body correlations, i.e. chemical short-range order or local distortions.

PDF

The PDF is the Fourier tranform of the total scattering diffraction pattern.2
