Principles of Resonant inelastic X-ray Scattering

Posted on 12-03-2022 Edited on 12-04-2022 In Core level spectrum , RIXS

X-ray Resonant Inelastic Scattering (RIXS) is an emerging excitation spectroscopy technique developed over the past decade. Leveraging the coupling of photons with the majority of degrees of freedom that determine material properties—including lattice, charge, spin, and orbitals—RIXS spectra can observe various excitations such as phonons, magnons, orbitons, and electronic level transitions, thereby providing rich physical information. Additionally, RIXS inherits the advantages of X-ray techniques, allowing its application in the measurement of low-dimensional materials and small samples. These benefits have positioned RIXS technology as a valuable tool in fields such as condensed matter physics, materials science, chemistry, and biology.

XAFS Theory

Posted on 07-10-2022 Edited on 01-17-2023 In Core level spectrum , XAFS

XAFS is an element-specific spectroscopy in which measurements are made by tuning the X-ray energy at and above a selected core-level binding energy of a specified element. Although XAFS is a well-established technique providing reliable and useful information about the chemical and physical environment of the probe atom, its requirement of an energytunable X-ray source means it is primarily done with synchrotron radiation sources and so is somewhat less common than other spectroscopic analytical methods. XAFS spectra are especially sensitive to the oxidation state and coordination chemistry of the selected element.

Principles of Powder Diffraction

Posted on 06-25-2020 Edited on 07-25-2023 In Pattern , Powder Diffraction

Powder diffraction is an awesome technique using X-ray, neutron, or electron diffraction on powder or microcrystalline samples for structural characterization of materials. Single crystal analysis is the preferred method for determining crystal structures, but when suitable single crystals are unavailable, powder diffraction is an effective alternative. Unlike single crystal analysis, powder diffraction also provides information on bulk material and microstructural properties. Nowadays, Powder diffraction continues to be a crucial tool in the characterization of materials.
